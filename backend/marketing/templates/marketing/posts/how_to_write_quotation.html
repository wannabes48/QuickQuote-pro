When you run a service business, your quotation is often the first formal document a prospective client sees. A professional quotation doesn't just tell them the price—it demonstrates your competence, builds trust, and clearly outlines the scope of work.

Why a Professional Quotation Matters

Many contractors still write quotes on scraps of paper or send quick WhatsApp messages with a single price. While this might be fast, it screams "amateur." Clients are looking for reliability. A well-formatted, detailed quote shows that you take your business seriously, which makes them feel safer trusting you with their project.

Essential Elements of a Winning Quotation

1. Your Business Details

Always include your company name, logo, contact information, and business address. If you are a registered business, include your tax number (e.g., KRA PIN). This establishes legitimacy.

2. Client Details

Make sure you have the correct name, phone number, and address of the client. This personalization shows you are paying attention to their specific needs.

3. Quote Number and Date

Every quote should have a unique identifier (like QQ-001) and an issue date. You should also include a "Valid Until" date to protect yourself against material price increases down the line.

4. Detailed Itemization

This is where most quotes fail. Never just write "Bathroom Remodel - $5,000." Break it down:

  • Labor: Number of hours or days, and the rate.
  • Materials: Specific brands or types of materials being used (e.g., "Moen Faucets" instead of just "Faucets").
  • Equipment Rental: If you need to rent a lift or special tools, list it.

5. Clear Terms and Conditions

When is payment due? Do you require a 50% deposit upfront? Are there any warranties on the work? Stating these terms clearly in the quotation prevents disputes when it's time to invoice.
